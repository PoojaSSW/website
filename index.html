About

My skillset and my approach on the design process.

Hello! I'm Pooja Wagh, a UI Engineer and an aspiring UX Designer/Engineer based in San Fracisco. I started my career as a UI engineer, however over the course of time, I realized that apart from translating the designs into a practical output, it is equally important to understand the thought process and the concepts and reasons behind all the designs by understanding and analyzing the needs of the users.

I discovered my insistent curiosity for design when I provided a helping hand to our UX team in building a feature for our product. I decided to explore by walking into the shoes of the users and getting a complete hold of what is cooking inside their heads.I’m here to bring smart ideas and solutions to the problems and show creativity in my designs.

My approach in developing a design strategy is to gain an empathic understanding of the problem to be solved and to find a balance between design that is action oriented, user-centered and solution focused towards the desired expectation. There are so many paths to take when solving a problem, and the ambiguity of the design process pushes me to discover the user’s psychology.

These are my ideal thoughts on tackling complex problems:

  • Emphasizing
    Understanding the human needs involved
  • Defining
    Defining the problem in human-centric ways
  • Iterative
    Creating many ideas in Iterative sessions
  • Prototyping
    Developing a prototype/solution to the problem

Design Process

Take a peek of my journey from gathering requirements to design and to development.

Requirements Gathering
I follow this practice as it helps me understand the software requirements and users problem space by using a set of techniques that provide me the information with the data. It tells me what and why behind the building of any task and ultimately suggests the best ways to capture the user requirements.
Design
Designing interfaces is all about finding improved ways to mediate how the user accomplishes a task. It may require that we create a whole new system. Or it may be that we simply design novel inputs and outputs.Design is not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works. Every user will be different and hence it is necessary to use an iterative and adaptable design process that fits within the users methodology and requirements.
Having a design process gives me an idea on how to best tackle a problem I’m trying to solve by consulting experts to find out more about the area of concern through observing, engaging and empathizing with users to understand their needs, and finally breaking it down into a series of interconnected systems to gain a deeper understanding of the issues involved.
My design process is divided into 5 way that  efficiently allows me to capture the user design requirement.

  • User Requirements

    - Gather user needs. Know who the customer is today and how would it be different in the next 5-10 years.
    - Define problem space. Know how the product will fit into the overall strategy.
    - Research for biggest competitors in the market.
    - How to meet business needs.
    - Who will be using this feature.

  • Brainstorming

    - Put together the information gathered to establish features, functions, and any other elements that will allow to solve the problems.
    - Get involved with the stakeholders and ask them to shout out ideas, encouraging them to be active in the process.
    - Explore alternative ideas based on further knowledge and technologies.
    - Record solutions.
    - Get the ideas rolling on a piece of paper or sketch it out.

  • Design

    - Break down the task into smaller, actionable goals.
    - Determine the type(s) of materials from which the solutions will be constructed.
    - Research for existing UX patters.
    - Prepare a design that is simple and user friendly than unique.
    - Create wireframes based on the components.

  • Prototyping

    - Determine the start to end state of every action.
    - Iterate over to identify any missed edge cases.
    - Build interactive prototypes for the user and the team to demonstrate the design in motion.
    - Reexamine or improve the prototype based on the feedback received.
    - Get an idea of constraints that are inherent to the product and the problems that are present.

  • Testing

    - Test the complete product from start to finish.
    - Finalize the components to handover to the engineers.
    - Collaborate with engineers to identify any technical constraints/challenges in replicating the design.

Mood Boards

This mood board is based upon the online food delivery app to get a general idea about the flow and usage of that app.

A mood board is a type of collage consisting of images, text, and samples of objects in a composition. It can be based upon a set topic or can be any material chosen at random. A mood board can be used to convey a general idea or feel about a particular topic. They may be physical or digital, and can be effective presentation tools.

GOAL

I had develop these mood boards to get some inspirational thoughts for my online food delivery app. Creating mood boards helped me formulate a theme that I wanted to convey for the online food delivery app project. The board provides an outlet for precise planning and organizing to trim time in the beginning design phases.
I used some of the creative website to get pictures of the food and placed them as a collage. It helped me explore variety of styles in photography, color palette, typography and overall look and feel of the site. The moodboard gave an idea for a quick built-in of the food listing dartboards I created for my food delivery app.

PeopleLive Chat App

An application that allows to communicate within a group of users or on one-on-one basis..

PeopleLive Chat is a simple application for a user or a group of users to chat in a room. This could involve all the stakeholders working on certain product, a quick way of communication your thought process and sharing your ideas/opnions on an internal forum.
The goal of the app is to take in a username to allow someone to "login", and then see a set of chat rooms with available messages. When clicked into a room, the user can view past messages, as well as add new messages.

Login screen
The first screen is the "login" screen. A user can type a username/email that they'll use to communicate with others in the room, once they provide some string and hit "Join the Peoplelive chat!” button, the username gets saved for any future messages that the user wants to send to other in the group chat.

Post-Login screen
When a username is submitted, the chat interface gets loaded with that user as a primary user in the chat room. THe user needs to select a room/group that he wants to communicate with. Once selected, he will be able to view all his previous messages and other messages related to that group.

TakeOut Online Food Delivery App

Efficient online resuorce for all food delivery services.

The TakeOut app provides a comprehensive list of restaurants that have great reviews, reliable delivery times, real photos of the products. The user has an option to input his address manually by using a field or can activate his location feature through his mobile that will automatically fill in the address. The user can skip this step and go to the default listing. If user wants he can register at the initial screen else when he orders the app will ask him to create an account.

Scenarios:

1. User is not logged in, the app will display the city with most restaurants.
2. User is logged, the dropdown will be a list of his addresses.

The app provides a rating system where users can rate and leave a comment. When a product is selected, it gets added on to the cart. Once the user decides his order, his credit card information will be needed. Once entered and hit next, the user has the ability to review the order. Once he is happy with his order, he can place the order and the final screen shows the approximate time of delivery and the total cost of the order.
